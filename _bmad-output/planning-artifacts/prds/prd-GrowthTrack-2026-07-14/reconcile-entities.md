# Reconciliation — entities.md vs PRD

Source: `_bmad-output/specs/spec-growthtrack/entities.md` (Phase 1 Data Entities: User, Notification, SalesData, BrandPerformance, Doctor; Notes on v2 proposals and open modeling questions)
Checked against: `prd.md` (§3 Glossary, §4 Functional Requirements, §13 Open Questions) and `addendum.md`.

## Covered

- **User** — `Role` field preserved and explicitly kept queryable for routing (FR-2); `Mobile` implied consistently in delivery-failure/retry narrative (UJ-2, UJ-3, FR-7) without being renamed or contradicted. `Opt-In Consent` (FR-10) is a genuinely new field not in entities.md's User entity, but it is explicitly tagged `[ASSUMPTION]` and traced to addendum §A3 — an added field handled transparently, not silently invented.
- **Doctor** — `Name` and `Territory` carried through verbatim in Glossary, FR-5, and §9 Data Governance ("Doctor entity is limited to name, territory, and target priority"). `DoctorID` is appropriately omitted from the Glossary/FR field lists, consistent with entities.md's own note that IDs/types belong to architecture, not this document.
- **SalesData** — `Date`/`SalesAmount` are aggregated into Today's/YTD/MTD Sales (FR-3) as computed values rather than invented raw fields. `Achievement` and `Growth` map directly to Achievement %/Growth %, with formulas correctly left as `[ASSUMPTION]` (Open Question 2) rather than silently resolved. `Team` field usage is consistent with "Sales Team" and is explicitly the subject of Open Question 7.
- **BrandPerformance** — `BrandName` and `Sales`/`Growth` are carried into FR-4's required list fields ("Brand Name, Sales, and Growth"). `BrandID` is appropriately omitted from Glossary/FR text, consistent with other entities' ID fields.
- **Notification** — `Recipient`, `Status`, and `SentTime` map cleanly onto FR-11's "recipient," "delivery status," and "date, time" fields; `Message` underlies FR-8's "compose a custom message." The Scheduled/Manual distinction is introduced as a feature-level concept (not baked silently into the entity schema), consistent with the still-open Notification/NotificationLog question.
- **Forecast** (entities.md Notes, v2/Phase 2 proposal) is correctly excluded from the Phase 1 PRD and explicitly pointed to `roadmap-phase2.md` (§5 Non-Goals) — neither silently dropped nor silently pulled forward into Phase 1.
- **Open modeling questions carryover** — Both open questions from entities.md's Notes section (whether `Team` needs to be a standalone entity vs. a field on `SalesData`/`User`; whether `Notification` should split into a template/history pair with `NotificationLog`) are carried into PRD §13 Open Question 7 nearly verbatim, explicitly deferred to architecture, and cross-reference `entities.md` by name — not silently resolved, not silently dropped.

## Gaps

- **BrandPerformance.Rank is dropped without acknowledgment.** entities.md lists `Rank` as a BrandPerformance field; FR-4's required brand-list fields are only "Brand Name, Sales, and Growth." `Rank` isn't carried forward, isn't flagged as superseded by the Top/Low-Performing/Focus classification, and isn't listed as a dropped field anywhere (§9 Assumptions Index or Open Questions).
- **Doctor.Priority is silently renamed to "Target Priority."** entities.md states fields are "named as in the source" and lists the field as `Priority`. The PRD (Glossary, FR-5, §9) uses "Target Priority" throughout with no `[ASSUMPTION]` tag or note acknowledging this is a rename rather than the source-preserved name — a silent naming divergence from the input this PRD is being reconciled against.
- **Recipient Group / Recipient Channel have no entities.md counterpart.** FR-9 and FR-12 require these as CRUD-managed, audit-logged directory objects alongside Users and Sales Teams, but entities.md's five Phase 1 entities contain no such concepts at all — these may originate in SPEC.md's CAP-1 rather than entities.md, but nothing in entities.md itself accounts for them, so they can't be reconciled against this specific input.
- **Team's "open" framing is narrower in practice than stated.** FR-9 (add/edit/remove Sales Teams) and FR-12 (audit-log Team CRUD with actor/timestamp) functionally require Team to have its own identity and lifecycle — which in practice presupposes something close to a standalone entity. Open Question 7 still presents "does Team need to be standalone" as fully open; worth flagging that the FRs already lean the answer one direction.
